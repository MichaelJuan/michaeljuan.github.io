on respecting wood

Nov 16, 2020
2 min read

Trying to type a quick unedited thought everyday/

Wood is an inherently impresice medium, it expands and contracts based on humidity and temperature. Depending on the sawing from the lumber yard wood can bow, crook, kink, cup, and dance. Additionally the strength of wood is heavily dependent on grain direction. I was walking today when I saw a nutcracker. The nutcracker had his head attached with dowel pins. The grain direction on the head above the jawline was forty five degrees off from the vertical axis and glued down sloppily. The head was then coated with a layer of paint and sprayed on lacquer. the legs also did not respect the grain direction of the wood.

After stopping to take a snapshot I kept walking. If some kid were to put a nut (any shelled nut will do) in the jaws of the nutcracker they would be in for a surprise. What would happen first would be the dowel pins shearing. The upper portion of the head would separate causing the Nutcracker to stare and groan. Then the kid would freak out having destroyed a public display with a do not touch/play sign. They would inevitably knock over the nutcracker causing the end grain on end grain glued miter joint to break. After a scolding from a parent the kid would lose the spark in their eyes and treat wooden products as if they were paper mache. If made correctly wood lasts longer than steel, concrete, or even plastic. Wood can take stress, strain, exposure to the elements (to a certain extent). But as long as we create inferior products that do not respect the grain direction and its natural expansion and contraction. We will have overconstrained nutcracker heads flying everywhere. Will go back and check on the nutcracker tomorrow, its probably fine since there are no nuts around it.
